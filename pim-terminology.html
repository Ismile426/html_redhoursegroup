📖 PIM in plain words

Product Information Management (PIM) is the single source of truth for all product data — descriptions, specs, media, pricing rules and channel-readiness — before it reaches any channel, ERP or storefront.

🏗️ Core PIM concepts
📦

Product Master / Family

The central record that holds all shared data for a product group. Never sold on its own — it is the recipe from which variants are made.

RedHorse: Template / Range (L3) e.g. 40400
🔩

SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)

A unique, buyable item with its own price, stock, and barcode. One SKU = one concrete sellable thing.

RedHorse: Variant (L4) e.g. 404001
🧬

Product Variant

A specific combination of dimension values (size, colour, drive type) that makes one SKU distinct from another within the same family.

RedHorse: 404001 vs 404002 (different Ø/length)
🗂️

Catalog / Taxonomy

A structured folder tree that organises products into categories. Every product belongs to at least one node. Drives search, navigation and channel exports.

RedHorse: Screws, Rivets, Hammers…
🏷

Attribute

A single piece of product information — e.g. "Head Type = CSH", "Length = 38 mm". Attributes are typed (text, number, select, boolean) and can be fixed or per-variant.

RedHorse: Fixed spec + configurable pools
📋

Attribute Set / Template

A named collection of attributes assigned to a product family. All products in the family share the same attribute set, ensuring consistent data structure.

RedHorse: Fixed + configurable keys on each range
🔢

Product Dimensions

The axes that generate variants: Configuration, Colour, Size, Style. Each combination of dimension values creates one unique SKU or sub-variant suffix.

RedHorse: Drive Type, Ø, Length + add-on Config/Colour/Size/Style
🌐

Channel / Storefront

The destination where product data is published — a webshop, marketplace, print catalogue, or ERP. PIM enriches once, then distributes to many channels.

RedHorse: DK, SE, US, EC storefronts

⚖️ PIM vs ERP — who owns what
PIM and ERP each own a distinct slice of product data. PIM is the master of what the product is; ERP is the master of what it costs and where it lives.
