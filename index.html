3 · Make-to-order vs make-to-inventory

The same product, ordered three ways

Make-to-Inventory · from history The bestseller, already on the shelf: a customer orders 200 wood screws, 7.2 × 180, TX25, Carbon, no washer — a popular version the planner pre-packed to stock from past sales.
  • 1Open Sales order; pick Wood screw 317 → Configure → 7.2 · 180 · TX25 · Carbon · Washer No.
  • 2This exact combo was built before → F&O reuses the existing Configuration (no duplicate).
  • 3Stock is on-hand → the line reserves from inventory. No production order.
  • 4Enter qty; price from trade agreement; confirm → pick, pack, ship from stock (fast).
Make-to-Inventory · seasonal forecast Pre-built for an expected busy season: before summer, the planner expects the stainless decking screw (5.0 × 60, A4) to sell heavily — even with thin history. So they pre-build it to stock now, ahead of orders.
  • 1No order yet — a planning decision based on the coming season.
  • 2The planner picks the version → its Configuration ID (built once, reused later).
  • 3A production order packs a batch to stock now — made to inventory, driven by the forecast.
  • 4When the season hits, orders ship from stock — no waiting for production.
Make-to-Order Built on demand: a builder wants 500 wood screws, 7.2 × 180, TX25, stainless A4, with washer — a rarer combo not kept in stock.
  • 1Add a line → pick Wood screw 317 → Configure: 7.2 · 180 · TX25 · A4 · Washer Yes (solver hides surfaces A4 can't take).
  • 2Finish → a new Configuration 317-7.2mm-180mm-TX25-PG; weight auto-filled.
  • 3Enter qty 500; price from the trade agreement; confirm.
  • 4Not in stock → confirming generates a production BOM & route and the screw is made to order (longer lead time).

4 · Sell in many companies (release)

Build once, release where you need

You build the rules once — each company just adds its own price, stock & brand. You never rebuild the configurator per company; you release the product to the companies that should sell it.
  • 1Build the model once on the wood-screw master.
  • 2Approve → Activate the version.
  • 3Release products → tick the companies (DK, SE, US…). Each gets its own “released product”.
  • 4In each company, set its own price, unit, tax, posting & costing group.
  • 5A salesperson in any of those companies can Configure and sell it — with that company’s own price, stock & brand.
Real example — the same screw, three companies. Release item 317 to DK, SE, US. Same physical screw & rules; only brand, price and stock differ:
